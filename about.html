About This Project

Combining machine learning and geospatial analysis to identify and address lead contamination sources that threaten millions of children in South Asia.

The Lead Contamination Crisis

Lead exposure is one of the most significant environmental health threats globally, affecting an estimated 500 million children. The World Health Organization identifies lead as one of the top ten environmental health risks, with no safe level of exposure.

Impact on Children

  • Neurotoxicity: Blood lead levels above 5 µg/dL are associated with reduced IQ, learning disabilities, and behavioral problems
  • Developmental delays: Exposure during critical developmental windows (0-3 years) causes permanent cognitive impairment
  • Cardiovascular effects: High lead exposure increases blood pressure and cardiovascular disease risk in adulthood
  • Economic burden: Lead-related health costs exceed $1 trillion annually in lost productivity and medical expenses

Sources of Lead Exposure in South Asia

While lead paint and gasoline additives remain concerns, informal industrial operations pose the greatest acute risk in South Asia:

Informal ULAB Recycling

Uncontrolled lead-acid battery recycling operations recover valuable lead but lack basic environmental controls. Workers and nearby residents experience severe lead exposure.

Widespread across South Asia, exact numbers unknown

Industrial Smelters

Both registered and unregistered smelter facilities process lead ore and scrap. Emissions contaminate soil, water, and air across wide areas.

Numerous facilities across target regions, many unregistered

Secondary Contamination

Lead-contaminated dust and soil spread from industrial sites into residential areas, schools, and food production zones.

Children exposed through soil ingestion and food consumption

About Pure Earth

Pure Earth (formerly Blacksmith Institute) is an international environmental organization dedicated to solving the world's most pressing pollution problems. For over 20 years, Pure Earth has worked with communities in developing countries to identify, assess, and remediate toxic sites that threaten public health.

Core Mission

Pure Earth's Toxic Sites Identification Program (TSIP) combines fieldwork, community engagement, and scientific research to:

  • Identify locations of lead contamination sources
  • Assess health risks to affected populations
  • Support remediation and facility closure efforts
  • Advance occupational health and safety improvements
  • Build community capacity for pollution reduction

Global Presence

Pure Earth operates in more than 40 countries, with regional programs in South Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, and the Western Balkans. The organization works with local partners, government agencies, and communities to address persistent pollution threats.

Learn more: Visit www.pureearth.org for more information about Pure Earth's programs and impact.

Better Planet Lab

The Better Planet Lab at the University of Colorado Boulder is a research group focused on applying machine learning and geospatial technology to environmental and public health challenges. The lab works across multiple domains including air quality, water pollution, biodiversity monitoring, and toxic site identification.

Our Approach

The lab combines expertise in:

  • Machine Learning: Deep learning, time series analysis, spatial modeling, active learning
  • Geospatial Analysis: Remote sensing, GIS, spatial statistics, environmental mapping
  • Data Engineering: Big data processing, cloud computing, data pipelines, API development
  • Domain Science: Environmental health, public health, chemistry, environmental engineering

TSIP Collaboration

The Better Planet Lab developed the machine learning models powering this screening tool in partnership with Pure Earth. Our goal is to make environmental health research accessible and actionable, enabling faster identification of contamination sources and more effective interventions.

University: University of Colorado Boulder

Impact & Next Steps

What We've Built So Far

  • Developed two complementary ML pipelines: contextual (XGBoost) for ULAB and satellite imagery (deep learning) for smelters
  • Completed proof-of-concept in NCR Delhi with 188 high-risk cells identified and 13 verified ULAB sites matched
  • Trained models on limited ground-truth data using active learning, demonstrating generalization from small datasets
  • Built interactive screening dashboard for Pure Earth field teams
  • Established data pipeline using OpenStreetMap, Sentinel-2, WorldPop, and Pure Earth's TSIP database

Current Limitations & Future Directions

While the models are effective, they represent a screening tool, not a complete solution. Future work includes:

  • Expansion: Scale models to additional regions (Mumbai, Kolkata, Southeast Asia)
  • Improvement: Incorporate new data sources, refine feature engineering, enhance model architectures
  • Real-time monitoring: Develop continuous screening for emerging sites and activity changes
  • Community science: Enable citizen scientists to contribute field observations and ground truth data
  • Multi-hazard detection: Extend models to detect other toxic sources (e-waste, tanneries, mining)
  • Health integration: Link site predictions with health outcome data (blood lead levels, developmental metrics)

Goal: Help Pure Earth and partner organizations systematically identify toxic sites at scale, so field teams can focus their limited resources on the highest-risk locations.
