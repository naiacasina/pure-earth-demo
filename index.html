Mapping Lead Contamination with Machine Learning

A screening tool for Pure Earth's Toxic Sites Identification Program. Using advanced machine learning models to detect informal lead-acid battery recycling operations and industrial smelters across South Asia.

How It Works

Contextual Model

Our XGBoost model analyzes geospatial and socioeconomic features to identify likely informal lead-acid battery (ULAB) recycling sites. Features include battery shop density, scrap metal dealers, population density, and proximity to residential areas.

Proof of concept: NCR Delhi — 188 high-risk cells, 13 verified sites matched

Satellite Imagery Model

A deep learning CNN analyzes multispectral Sentinel-2 imagery to detect industrial smelter facilities. The model identifies spatial patterns, building morphology, land cover context, and spectral characteristics associated with smelter operations.

Training data: USGS + ILZSG geolocated smelter records

Risk Prioritization

Each detected site receives a risk score from 0.0 to 1.0 based on model confidence and contextual factors. Sites are prioritized for field verification based on risk level, ensuring resources focus on the highest-impact interventions.

About This Initiative

Lead contamination from informal recycling operations and industrial smelters poses a severe public health threat, particularly to children in South Asia. There is no safe level of blood lead — even low concentrations are associated with developmental delays, learning disabilities, and long-term health problems.

This Toxic Sites Identification Program combines machine learning with ground-truth data to help identify and prioritize these high-risk locations. By screening vast geographic areas with remote sensing and contextual analysis, we enable Pure Earth and partner organizations to focus limited field verification resources on the most likely sites.

The dashboard presented here demonstrates the screening capability using real model outputs and verified site locations from NCR Delhi. This tool is designed to support evidence-based intervention planning and environmental health decision-making.
